Why GroundWork exists

The problem isn't lack of ideas.

It's that environments resist change differently — and most teams only discover how after they've committed.

Advice travels faster than adaptation

Modern product and AI advice spreads quickly through podcasts, newsletters, social feeds, and startup culture. Teams absorb the language long before they absorb the operating conditions required to make it work.

Move faster. Become AI-first. Empower teams. Experiment more. Reduce process. Ship prototypes. Adopt founder mode. The prescriptions are familiar. The implementation rarely is.

Organizations don't fail transformations because they lack vision.
They fail because incentives, coordination, trust, and pressure reshape behavior in ways the advice didn't anticipate.

What actually gets in the way

  • Incentives that resist the change you're trying to make
  • Decision structures that conflict with distributed ownership
  • Coordination overhead that grows with velocity
  • Review systems that lag behind shipping speed
  • Trust systems that don't evolve with new tools
  • AI shifting bottlenecks faster than roles adjust
  • Pressure changing what people optimize for — quietly

Vision → Operational Reality

GroundWork exists to bridge that gap. Not as a scorecard or a consultant's framework — as a calm mirror for how modern organizations actually behave when they try to change.

We draw on recurring operational patterns from product leadership conversations — including themes explored across Lenny's Podcast and Newsletter — not to cite episodes, but to reflect what keeps showing up in real companies under pressure.
